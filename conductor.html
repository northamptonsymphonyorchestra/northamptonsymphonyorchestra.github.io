Northampton Symphony Orchestra

Information for Candidates for the post of Musical Director: 


Northampton Symphony Orchestra is looking for a Musical Director/Conductor to take up post in September 2015.  We are looking to appoint a talented musician who has experience of conducting amateur orchestras.  We would expect the incumbent to maintain and develop the orchestra further with inspirational rehearsals and rewarding concerts.

The orchestra’s repertoire is wide and varied and we perform at various venues in Northampton.  We are essentially a community orchestra where the standard is Grade 8 and above.  The orchestra is a registered charity and is run by a committee of member trustees.   Throughout the school term the orchestra rehearses on a Wednesday from 7.30-9.45pm with an afternoon rehearsal on the day (Saturday or Sunday) of the concert.  All rehearsals are taken by the Musical Director and they would be expected to plan a rehearsal schedule for each concert run with provision for sectional rehearsals as appropriate.

We intend to interview a list of candidates who will then be shortlisted and invited to rehearse and guest conduct one of our concerts during the period January 2015 to June 2015.

Subsequently shortlisted candidates will be expected to rehearse and guest conduct one of the following concerts for a mutually agreed fee:

28 February 2015 (Rehearsals 7, 14, 21, 28 Jan and 4, 11, 25 Feb)
WA Mozart - Overture – Cosi fan tutti
Robert Schumann - Piano Concerto, Opus 54 in A Minor
Anton Bruckner - Symphony no. 6 in A Major

18 April 2015 (Rehearsals 4, 11, 18, 25 Mar and 1, 15 Apr)
Antonin Dvorak  - Midday Witch, opus 108
Richard Strauss - Oboe Concerto
Sergei Rachmaninoff - Symphony no. 1 opus 13 in D minor

13 June 2015 (Rehearsals 22, 29 Apr; 6, 13, 20 May and 3, 10 Jun)
William Walton - Spitfire Prelude and Fugue
Max Bruch - Scottish Fantasy, opus 46
Arnold Bax  -Tintagel
Ralph Vaughan Williams - Symphony No 6 in E minor

If you are interested in applying for this post please send an email to Katy Osborne, NSOrecruitment99@gmail.com

1.    Copy of your CV
2.    A covering letter addressing the skills and experience as set out in the person specification for the post
3.    Fee expectations
4.    Confirm which concert and rehearsal runs you can make

The closing date for applications is 30th September 2014

Interviews will be held on Sunday 12th October 2014 in Northampton.  We regret we will be unable to enter into dialogue with candidates not invited to interview.  We intend to conclude this process and confirm the position of conductor by end of June 2015.

Person Specification

•    A musician with excellent musical qualifications and experience of working with amateur and professional musicians at the highest level
•    Experience of rehearsing and conducting amateur orchestra containing members with differing levels of experience so rehearsals are motivating and enjoyable.
•    Wide knowledge of the orchestral repertoire
•    Ability to work with the Orchestra’s committee to plan and prepare exciting and enjoyable concerts for both orchestra and audience.
•    Ability to inspire and excite the Orchestra to tackle unfamiliar and challenging music.

Role Description

•    Be responsible for the development of musical standards of the Orchestra.
•    To ensure the highest possible musical standards are achieved both at rehearsals and concerts, consistent with the Orchestra’s musical and financial resources.
•    Take all rehearsals on a Wednesday evening during school term time and undertake additional rehearsals where necessary.
•    Conduct all concerts in a season.
•    To make recommendations to the committee for the engagement of soloists.
•    To advise and assist the committee in the management of the orchestra as required.
•    Provide a written schedule at the start of each concert, including sectional rehearsals as appropriate.
•    Help plan the programme for future seasons in collaboration with the committee.
